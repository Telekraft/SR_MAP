How It Works

Arrival in the car park

The traveller reserves their parking place in advance online. Upon arrival, an available dedicated drop-off spot is ready and waiting at the entrance of the car park to drop off the vehicle. Once the car is locked, the car owner validates their reservation at the computer terminal provided for this purpose. They are now free to leave to catch their flight. A robot takes over the task of parking their car in a secure car park.

Exiting the car park

While validating the reservation, we will have recovered information pertaining to the traveller‚Äôs return flight. As our technology is connected to real-time flight information, we know exactly when the user is going to land. The robot prepares the vehicle a few minutes before the scheduled arrival, placing it in a box, ready to exit the car park.

Parking capacity

Capacity in a conventional car park

In a conventional car park, the average space is approximately 25m2 in size. The overall organisation of the space is designed to allow users to move about, manoeuvre and retain access to their cars (boot, doors).

In a Stanley Robotics car park

With Stanley Robotics, no space is needed for users to manoeuvre about. Our robots move and park the cars in order to maximise all the space available in the car park. We can, therefore, create an approximate 50% increase in the number of spaces.

Car park entrance/exit

Entering and exiting a conventional car park

The experience of entering and exiting a conventional car park is often tedious for the user. Before parking, motorists must wait in front of a boom barrier, take a ticket, and then spend time looking for an available space.

In a Stanley Robotics car park

Stanley Robotics simplifies the parking experience, eliminating the need for this barrier, and eliminating the drivers‚Äô need to search for a free space. Having already reserved in advance online, the client simply parks their car in a spacious dedicated drop-off spot that is waiting for them at the entrance. On their return, their vehicle is waiting for them once more, ready to go.

Maximum walking distance

In a conventional car park

Generally long-term car parks are located far away from airport terminals. It is common for the user to have to park a few kilometres away, walk several minutes, and then take a shuttle back to the terminal. For airports, the implementation of this transport system is very expensive.

In a Stanley Robotics car park

With Stanley Robotics, we remove all the walking inside the car park. Thanks to the boxes, the motorist leaves their car at the entrance and heads straight to the terminal, be it via a nearby shuttle, or, in some cases, directly on foot.

Shuttle Bus Stops

In a conventional car park

To enable users easy access to the terminals, airports need to deploy a fleet of shuttles and establish a network of stops distributed around the car park. For example, in some airports, there can easily be up to 15 different designated stops.

In a Stanley Robotics car park

With Stanley Robotics, only one shuttle stop is necessary. This not only simplifies terminal access, but also reduces shuttle travel time, which saves travellers time when catching their flight. In some cases (when the terminal is nearby), there is no need for a shuttle.

Cabins / Kiosks

Spacious and well-lit dedicated drop-off spots

We have created dedicated spots at the car park entrances for users to come and drop off their car. These spacious and well-lit spots are designed to improve user experience and minimise the time necessary to get to their flight.

A computer terminal to simplify the experience

A computer terminal is located on each side of the spot. This individual interface allows the user to confirm their reservation and flight information in just a few clicks, allowing us, upon their return, and at the right time, to prepare their car for pick up.

Security

In a conventional car park

In a conventional car park, it is practically impossible to control human comings and goings. Hence, your car is not safe from break-ins or the potential to be stolen. To counter these problems, airports must install security measures such as video surveillance and hire additional personnel.

In a Stanley Robotics car park

With Stanley Robotics, the interior of the car park is completely closed to humans, only the robots can move about. Because of this, the vehicles are always protected, thus eliminating offences, intrusions or complex security systems to safeguard the car park.

OPEX (OPERATING EXPENSES)

In a conventional car park

In a conventional car park, there are many operating costs required to maintain a quality level of service such as ground markings to indicate parking spaces and to guide users, lights for night-time access and navigation, cleaning the space to maintain a high level of cleanliness, security patrols, etc...

In a Stanley Robotics car park

With Stanley Robotics, car park operating costs are significantly reduced. As the car park is not accessible to users, there is no longer any need for ground markings, lighting or cleaning; the robots don‚Äôt require it.

CO2 Emissions

In a conventional car park

In a conventional car park, cars emit CO2 as they circulate, stop and speed up. Airports monitor their carbon footprint, and for many, it is a priority to lower their CO2 emissions index in the coming years.

In a Stanley Robotics car park

With Stanley Robotics, cars no longer circulate as engines are permanently off. Our robots, which run on eco-friendly electricity, move and park the cars.

Car manoeuvring and collisions

In a conventional car park

Conventional car park users are often confronted with dangerous and difficult driving situations. Car collisions frequently occur when parking. Upon their return, it is also a common irritation for car park users to find their car has been the victim of a collision.

In a Stanley Robotics car park

With Stanley Robotics, users have no manoeuvres to make. The dedicated drop-off spots are wide enough to make dropping off your car an easy task. Our robots take care of everything else. They are equipped with multiple sensors to ensure all manoeuvres, even complex ones, without risk of collision.

Luggage trolleys

In a conventional car park

To facilitate luggage transport in a conventional car park, many baggage trolleys need to be deployed in a variety of locations. For the user, it is difficult to know exactly where they may be found, and seeking them out is time wasted. For the airport, the trolleys must be replenished all day long and those left in the middle of the alleys collected.

In a Stanley Robotics car park

With Stanley Robotics, we centralise the trolleys directly in front of the dedicated drop-off spots. As they are positioned at the drop-off point, the traveller has hassle-free access to a trolley to carry any luggage they may have. This centralisation also simplifies airport management, allowing for simple one-stop replenishment and more available trolleys within the car park.

FLEXIBILiTY IN YOUR IMPLANTATION

Our technology is easy to implement and allows you to flexibly adjust your parking capacity depending on actual growth in passenger traffic. You just have to put a fence around a larger area of your current car park, and easily add some robots and/or cabins. We can thus create extra spaces according to your real needs, helping you manage and optimize costs. Conversely, if passenger flows decrease you can - thanks to robots - decrease the size of your car park even more, freeing real estate for an alternate use (AirportCity, Hotel, Golf course,..).
